• The statistical approaches that apply to non-repairable system or repairable system are different.
• For non-repairable system, probability/statistics typically requires IID (independent and identically distributed) 
assumption of failure data so we could use classical distributions (like normal, exponential, Weibull, etc.) to model 
the failure behavior. Under IID, the occurrence order of failure times has no significance.
• For repairable system, since the time between successive failures may not be IID anymore and the occurrence 
order of repairs is significant in affecting the future “interarrival times”, we need to use stochastic counting process 
to model the failure behavior.
